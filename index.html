All about kegging

Kegging is the process of loading your beer or cider into kegs after fermentation in order to carbonate without leaving the yeast alive. In bottling, the yeast will continue eating the sugar after bottling, which creates carbonation. However getting this process right is fairly hard. Too much fermentation in the bottle will cause explosions because of the pressure. Too little fermentation in the bottle will result in a sour taste. Kegging is the superior method of consuming your craft beer or cider.

Pin-lock or ball-lock

The type of keg you use is really a personal choice. You'll find that ball lock kegs are less available and more expensive, but there's no good reason for this to be so. I use pin-lock kegs and they were not only cheaper, but easier to find as well. The bottom line is that it is up to you!

Things you will need

  • Kegs
  • CO2
  • Beer or cider
  • Starsan
